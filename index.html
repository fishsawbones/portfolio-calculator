How This Works
♾️
Perpetuity Sizing
Portfolio = gross annual draw ÷ (net return − base inflation). This is the capital that sustains growing withdrawals forever under base-case assumptions. When the spread is too thin, a 40-year growing annuity is used instead.
🎯
Base = Perpetual by Design
The base case is what the portfolio was sized for. It should never deplete at your chosen return. If it does, the return is too low to support the income target indefinitely at 3% CPI + 1% lifestyle creep.
🎚️
Return = Tipping-Point Dial
Drag the return slider and watch scenarios cross from perpetual to depleting. The spread between net return and each scenario's inflation rate determines whether the portfolio survives. This is the most important slider.
📉
Sequence Risk Buffer
Adds capital above the perpetuity floor. A 10% buffer means 10% more than the theoretical minimum, lowering the initial withdrawal rate. Extra runway if markets start badly.
🏥
Healthcare Costs
Medical inflation weighted at 15% of total spending. Runs 1.5–2× CPI historically. The pessimistic and stress scenarios assume healthcare cost acceleration that compounds above CPI.
🏠
Lifestyle Creep
0.5%/yr optimistic to 2%/yr stress. Baked into total inflation. Even 1%/yr lifestyle creep roughly doubles your required withdrawal over 70 years. The compounding is what kills portfolios.
📐
Income Invariance
Depletion years do not change with income level. $100K/mo needs exactly 2× the portfolio of $50K/mo with identical trajectory. Only return, tax, inflation, and buffer shift timing.
⚠️
Stress = 1970s Analog
6.5% sustained CPI, aggressive healthcare growth, 2%/yr lifestyle expansion. At default 7% return, only stress depletes. Drop the return slider to 5% and watch even the base case break.
