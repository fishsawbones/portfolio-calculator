Factors Incorporated
📈
CPI Inflation
Optimistic 2% · Base 3% · Pessimistic 4.5% · Stress 6.5%. Post-pandemic structurally elevated baseline assumed in the base case.
🏥
Healthcare Costs
Medical inflation historically runs 1.5–2× CPI. Premium coverage for high-income households modeled at 5–9% annual increases. Weighted at 15% of total spend.
💸
Tax Drag
LTCG + NIIT federal rate of 23.8% on distributions. No Texas state income tax. Adjustable via slider for different portfolio structures.
📉
Sequence Risk
Early bear markets permanently impair a withdrawal portfolio. The buffer slider adds capital above the perpetuity floor to absorb a bad opening sequence.
🏠
Lifestyle Creep
High-income households increase real spending 0.5–2%/yr beyond CPI. Modeled as a separately capitalized addon — not hidden in the discount rate where it double-counts.
🏦
Withdrawal Framework
Classic 4% rule targets a 30-year horizon. True endowment-style preservation requires 2.5–3.5% real. Each scenario uses whichever approach applies given the real return.
♾️
Perpetual vs. Finite
When after-tax real return exceeds inflation, a perpetuity is used — principal preserved forever. Below that threshold, a finite-horizon growing annuity is used instead.
⚠️
Stagflation Stress
Stress scenario = 1970s analog: 6.5% sustained CPI, aggressive healthcare cost growth, 2%/yr lifestyle expansion. Designed as the worst-case survival test.
